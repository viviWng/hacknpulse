# Article 5 - Prohibited AI Practices
ARTICLE_5_PROHIBITED = """
Article 5 – Prohibited artificial intelligence practices

1. The following artificial intelligence practices shall be prohibited:
   (a) Placing on the market, putting into service or using an AI system 
       that deploys subliminal techniques beyond a person's consciousness 
       with the intention to materially distort a person's behaviour in a 
       manner that causes or is likely to cause harm;
   
   (b) Placing on the market, putting into service or using an AI system 
       for the purpose of social scoring on the basis of social behaviour 
       or personal characteristics with the potential to:
       (i) unfairly categorise or unfairly treat individuals or whole groups;
       (ii) have negative impacts on civically important activities;
   
   (c) Placing on the market, putting into service or using an AI system 
       that uses real-time remote biometric identification for the purpose 
       of law enforcement unless:
       (i) it is necessary to search for a specific identified person;
       (ii) it is necessary to prevent a specific imminent threat to life;
       (iii) special authorisations are in place through national law;
"""

# Article 6 - High-Risk AI Systems
ARTICLE_6_HIGH_RISK = """
Article 6 – Classification of high-risk AI systems

Annex I specifies the categories of AI systems that shall be classified as 
high-risk AI systems. These include:

- AI systems intended to be used for recruitment and selection of personnel
- AI systems intended to be used for evaluation of workers and job applicants
- AI systems intended to be used for assessment of academic performance
- AI systems intended to determine access to credit
- AI systems intended to assess creditworthiness
- AI systems used for identification of law enforcement data
- And other categories per Annex I

High-risk AI systems must comply with requirements for:
- Risk assessment and mitigation
- Data governance
- Human oversight
- Transparency and information
- Research and monitoring
"""

# Article 9 - Risk Assessment
ARTICLE_9_RISK_ASSESSMENT = """
Article 9 – Risk assessment

High-risk AI systems shall be subject to a risk assessment before being 
placed on the market or put into service. The risk assessment shall:

(a) Identify and analyse reasonably foreseeable risks;
(b) Assess the severity of such risks;
(c) Evaluate existing and proposed mitigation measures and controls;
(d) Document the risk assessment process and results;
(e) Be updated whenever significant changes occur.
"""

# Article 10 - Data Quality And Governance
ARTICLE_10_DATA_QUALITY = """
Article 10 – Data quality, governance and management

High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed such that:

(a) Training, validation and testing data sets are:
    (i) relevant, representative, error-free and complete
    (ii) adequate in terms of frequency and characteristics
    (iii) subject to documented data governance practices
    
(b) Data management processes are:
    (i) documented and effective
    (ii) subject to appropriate monitoring
    (iii) auditable
"""

# Article 13 - Transparency And Information To Users
ARTICLE_13_TRANSPARENCY = """
Article 13 – Transparency and information to users

Deployers of high-risk AI systems shall:

(a) Be informed that they are interacting with, or are subject to a decision 
    taken by, an AI system;
(b) Receive meaningful information about the AI system;
(c) Understand how the system works;
(d) Be made aware of the possibility to request human intervention;
(e) Be provided with information about their rights.

For AI systems that generate content (text, images, audio, video):
- Users shall be informed it was generated by AI
- Appropriate record-keeping shall be maintained
"""

# Article 14 - Human Oversight
ARTICLE_14_HUMAN_OVERSIGHT = """
Article 14 – Human oversight

High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed such that they can be 
effectively overseen by natural persons through:

(a) Automatic stopping of the execution of a specific task;
(b) Human intervention to override, adjust or reverse an AI decision;
(c) Intervention during the design, development and deployment phases;

Human oversight shall be meaningful and proportionate. Users shall have:
(a) Information to interpret model outputs
(b) Ability to exercise human decision-making
(c) Access to explanations of decisions
(d) Ability to challenge outcomes
"""
